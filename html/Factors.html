Factors

Environmental Factors

The antenna will be placed on the roof of EWI. This means that the antenna should be built such that it can survive and be used in this environment. To be more specific, this includes temperature, humidity, air pressure and flow, EMI, physical shocks and vibrations and illumination for example. The most important factors of these are temperature and EMI, as these have the largest impact on the actual functioning of the antenna.

The antenna is a whip antenna (short rod) with a length of 50cm. The antenna rod is assumed to be placed on a supporting structure. The interface is used to split/combine the supply power and the signal power and it is placed close to the antenna.

  • According to the application description, the antenna should be able to operate between $-25\,. \!. \!.+70^{\circ}$C
  • The input of the antenna should also be protected against ESD discharge
  • The -3dB frequency range of the active antenna should equal 10kHz to 30MHz. The 1dB compression level at the output of the amplifier, measured at the input of the receiver should be above +0dBm (0dBm =1mW in $50 \Omega$).
  • At the antenna input, this should be equivalent with an electrical field strength of $0.45V_{RMS}m^{-1}$

Cost Factors

  • Dimensions: The electronics for the active antenna should fit on a PCB of maximally 25 x 50 x 15 mm. The length of the antenna equals 50 cm and the diameter equals 5 mm.
  • Power Consumption: The power consumption of the antenna should 0.25 W maximum.
  • Additional costs:
    • Price of production
    • Materials used in production
    • Installation and maintenance

Functional Specifications

Electronics for the active antenna must be designed with the following functional specifications

  • The design must be compatible with an active whip antenna of 50cm length and 5mm diamter
  • The active antenna must receive power and communicate via the same 50 Ohm coax cable with SMA connectors which is connected to the interface
  • The supply voltage must be less than 10V DC.
  • The input of the active antenna should be protected against ESD discharge
  • The electronics should withstand temperatures between -25 and 70 degrees Celcius
  • The active antenna must contain an amplifier

Performance Specifications

Electronics for the active antenna must be designed with the following performance specifications

  • The power consumption should be less than 0.25W
  • The -3dB freguency range of the active antenna should be equal to 10kHz to 30MHz
  • The electrical field strength at the antenna input should be equivalent to 0.45 Vrms m^-1
  • The inaccuracy of the gain at frequencies between 100kHz and 1MHz should be less than 20%
  • The antenna-referred quivalent noise spectral density limits can be obtained by linear interpolation of the following list
